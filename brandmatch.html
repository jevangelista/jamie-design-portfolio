Brand Match

Switchfly Project:
Documenting a Design System and
Revamping the Client Theming & Branding Process

Overview

As a white label software, one of the most important parts of onboarding a new client at Switchfly is the Brand Match process — basically, applying a client’s brand styles to our design system and the UI components that make up their version of the application. In the past, the UX team's involvement in that process wasn't very clearly defined and, at that time, client styles were being captured via a Microsoft Word Document. One of my first projects at Switchfly was evaluating, revamping, and redesigning that process.

Project Goals

As the sole designer, my main goals for this project were to:

  1. Leverage Sketch and its symbols feature to create a tool that incorporates our design system and generates quick mockups that automatically applies client styles to save the design team time
  2. Generate documentation that would reduce back & forth with the client, as well as act as a smooth hand-off tool for our developers & QA
  3. Reduce extensive customization of our SAAS platform by educating & encouraging our clients to go with out-of-the-box options in order to reduce time and effort for our developers
  4. Help to develop a consistent, high-quality, and visually pleasing end-product to increase customer satisfaction and reduce the amount of bugs reported by them

Discover

Audit

I started the project by going through each page of our application and inspecting each element to see how styles were being applied, documenting those values every step of the way.

Next, I cross-referenced the values that I captured against the styles outlined in our pattern library and our standard app theming scss file, which is a code document that contains all the variables used to style a client’s website. Fun fact: There are over 16,000 unique variables in the apptheme.scss file that styles a given implementation.

Tool Exploration

Going into the project, my Creative Director and I knew we wanted to move away from the old Microsoft Word Document and build a tool that allowed us to be able to create documentation for clients & developers, and, at the same time, generate fast mockups.

Define

Following my audit, I worked together with one of our developers to confirm which variables from our app theme file matched with which component in our app. Next, I sifted through our client websites and each of their app theme files to determine which items were commonly overridden. From there, I listed and defined what items we wanted to highlight for our clients to be able to override out-of-the-box.

Sitemap

In order to determine how to organize the content within the Brand Match Sketch Tool, I created a sort of sitemap to lay out the different parts and sections in a way that made sense. We wanted to create a structure in which any designer from our team or from the client’s design team could jump in and understand how to use the tool and navigate through it.

Design

Iteration

The design tool went through multiple iterations (about over 20) until we came across a format that worked. The tool had to be intuitive enough that any designer on our team could take the Sketch file and understand how to use it, and possibly a designer from the client’s side, as well. Additionally, I came up with the instructional copy for the tool on my own, as well.

In Action

Basically, the Sketch file is divided 3 main parts:
  1. Brand styles document - a document consisting of different artboards, where the designer can see all the symbols and elements from our app that can be possibly updated
  2. Symbols page - the symbols section where the designer actually updates the style values (ie. hex values, font sizes, etc.)
  3. Page previews - mockups of key pages from our app in desktop and mobile views, which are built out of the symbols from the other sections

Output

After the designer applies the client's styles to Brand Match Sketch Tool, the following items will get generated:
  • Powerpoint document - that outlines how styles and overrides are mapped to the app for both the client and our own developers to refer to
  • Mockups of key pages - from our application with the client’s styles applied, which gives them a preview of their themed version of the site

Deliver

Ultimately, the end deliverable of this project was a Sketch file that would be re-used by our team everytime a client requested to have their version of the app go through a brand match. In addition to the Brand Match Sketch Tool, I worked together with one of our platform’s lead product managers to further define the UX design team’s role in the process.

Conclusion

Results

Overall, the Brand Match Sketch Tool helped to improve Switchfly’s brand match process through the following ways:
  • Cut the development time and effort down from an average of 1400 hours to 170 hours
  • Reduced the amount of design time from 2 days (~16 hours) down to ~4 hours
  • Successfully implemented the new tool and streamlined Brand Match process on 6 satisfied clients: Royal Orchid Holidays, PenFed (Meredian Loyalty), Aspire Lifestyles, Chase (Ritz-Carlton Rewards), Marriott Bonvoy, EasyTahiti

Some of the feedback we've received from clients:
  • “This is great. This is definitely leaps and bounds better than what we had to go through.”
  • “We don't want to have to put together mock-ups and set the wrong expectations with our own clients. Switchfly’s mock-ups will be better to make sure everything shown is feasible."
  • "Excited to show this to new clients."

Next Steps

If given more time on this project, I would take the following next steps:
  • Continue building out more of the components used in our app, since we continue to add new features and new pieces of UI with each release cycle
  • Further refine the copy in the powerpoint document and possibly consider creating different versions for each audience (developers, clients, other designers, etc.)
  • Look into a more interactive tool or way to present a client’s branded site that has a similar symbols feature as Sketch, as a step up from this Brand Match Sketch Tool
