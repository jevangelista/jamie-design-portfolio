Switchfly Initiative:
Continuous Improvements Projects

Overview

Recently, Switchfly began an initiative to help one of its clients American Express improve their RTF (refer-to-friend) score and conversion rates through a series of “continuous improvements” or small iterative stories using a rapid, agile approach. I was brought on to help on some of these projects, and below are some of examples of the work I’ve done. These solutions are the result of research, testing, and cross team communication, while balancing technical constraints, accessibility, and the other client considerations.

Flight Results Page Improvements

Summary

One of the requests that came from AMEX revolved around one of our flight shopping flows. Basically, they wanted to make some changes to one of our platform’s flight results pages. My hypothesis was that there were actually some larger usability issues on that page, so I proposed a different solution to the product manager on this project and luckily convinced him to get on board.

Adding Layover Information

Summary

Another request from AMEX involved adding layover information to the cards on our flight results page. A big challenge with this was, in addition to figuring out how to format and where to place the information, our application has 5 different flight shopping flows to take into account, all with widely varying layouts. Results

TripAdvisor Rating Filter

Summary

Previously, users didn’t have a way to filter for reviews on hotel results pages, so AMEX had requested that we add a filter for TripAdvisor ratings to our UI. Based on past user testing, we knew reviews were very important to the user when it came to shopping for hotels, so this seemed like a feature all of our clients could benefit from and possibly improve conversion.



Results

I conducted a user test to decide on what format to display the filter and where to place it on our filters panel. Ultimately, I decided to go with a checkbox format and placed it right under the Price Range filter. Based on user testing results, users felt the option was rather important and should be placed towards the top, and the checkbox format seemed to give them the right amount of control and flexibility.

Although, it was a small feature, this filter ended up having a big impact. It became the 4th most used hotel filter amongst all Switchfly clients and lead to a 2x higher conversion when used.
