Instashop

Designlab UX Academy Project:
An Online Grocery Shopping App

Overview

Background

Instashop is a fictitious grocery franchise based in the United States. Over the years, market shares have been decreasing most likely due to the increase in online grocery ordering & delivery products. The investors of Instashop believe there is a big market out there for online shopping and are determined to be onle of the major players in this page.

As the designer, I was tasked to understand who the customers are and problems in the current space in order to build a product that improves upon their online shopping experience.

Discover

I first created a research plan that centered around understanding the target audience and what their current shopping habits look like, as well as their motivations and frustrations when it comes to online shopping. I conducted user interviews with 3 participants and distributed an online survey.

Primary Research Findings

Highlighted below are key insights from my primary research:
  • The biggest frustrations participants had with grocery shopping included having to deal with crowds and stores not having products that they wanted.
  • When it came to choosing a grocery store to shop in, the following were the most important factors as voted by participants: variety of products, good prices, distance from their house, and quality of products.
  • Free and fast delivery appeared to be the most important factor when it came to what would make it easier for them to do their grocery shopping online.

Competitive Analysis

To arrive at a deeper understanding of the problem space, I conducted a competitive analysis to see how others are trying to solve the problem. I evaulated the following competitors: AmazonFresh, Instacar, and Good Eggs.

Define

Based on user research and data, I came up with a persona that represented the target demographic for Instashop — the young, tech-savvy, busy working professional. In order to understand and communicate the problems and mindset of our users, I created an empathy map that centered around this persona.

Business Goals

I outlined both the goals of the business and the end user to see where the two might overlap in order to provide some direction on where to focus moving forward.

Information Architecture

In order to help organize content and categories within the site, I conducted a card sorting exercise with users. Based on results, I created the sitemap below to show the relationship between different pieces of content within the site.

Following the sitemap, a user flow was created to illustrate how a user would complete each of the following tasks: arrives to homepage, finds items to buy; adds items to cart; and checks out, signs up in the process of checking out.

Design

Wireframes

Building off my sitemap and user flow chart, I drafted up some low-fidelity wireframes in Sketch that incorporated elements of common design patterns and illustrated the overall layout of the app.
Additionally, I created web-based wireframes for the site experience, which used the standard responsive design patterns. I created layouts for the different 'viewports' — desktop, table, and mobile — for the following pages: homepage, category page, and item detail page.

Test

I combined several of my wireframes and created a prototype first using the MarvelApp, then a more detailed one through InVision.
Video of prototype

Usability Testing

I conducted both in-person and remote user testing. In person, 3 participants were asked to complete the following tasks:
  1. Create an account with Instashop
  2. Navigate/browse through the app to get to an item detail page
  3. Add 1 item to the cart
  4. Proceed through the checkout process and successfully complete an order

Five tests were conducted remotely, with responses from a total of 34 responses. Through the Verify app, I carried out two remote tests: 1) a click test to see how a user navigates through the app and 2)a memory test to see what the user remembers about the item detail page. Through UsabilityHub, I carried out 3 tests — the first two similar to ones done through Verify and an additional preference test.

Affinity Map

I gathered my notes from testing and created an affinity map to help sort, prioritize, and rank user feedback.

Deliver

Brand Style

For the visual style, I wanted to take on a direction that was a little more fun, fresh, and unconventional. For the logo, I implemented a gif of multiple icons to emphasize the variety of product Instashop offered in a quirky kind of way. I went with the bold, geometric Futura font and a vibrant color palette, as well as stylized photography, to drive home the fun and fresh direction .

Final Handoff

Conclusion

Lessons Learned

This was a big project that taught me a lot about each step of the UX Design process. Most importantly, this project taught me the value of user research. For example, before I conducted my research, I had some presumptions about how the target demographic would look like. However, as I went through the research process, I discovered that the target audience was quite different than what I had originally drawn up from my provisional persona. Also, all the different empathy exercises, such as the empathy map and storyboarding, allowed me to really step into the shoes of the user. Building the persona and empathy for the user created a solid foundation that helped guide my design desions along the way.

Next Steps

If given more time on this project, I would take the following next steps:
  • Explore and create flows for other features including saving/favoriting an item and looking up a recipe
  • On the item detail page, A/B test a version showing a grid of personal recommondations of products vs. a grid of recipes
  • Create a quiz during sign up that would capture user preferences in order to help inform personalized recommendations
