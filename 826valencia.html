826 Valencia

Designlab UX Academy Project:
A Website Redesign for a Literary Non-Profit

Overview

Background

826 Valencia is a nonprofit organization dedicated to supporting students with their writing skills and helping teachers get their students excited about writing. The organization currently has a functioning site with strong branding and graphical elements, but the layout is quite dated, cluttered, and, most noticeably, isn't mobile optimized. As 826 Valencia continues to grow, the website needs to better accommodate mobile users and re-organize content to communicate their message more clearly.

My main goals of this capstone project were to:
  1. Redesign 826 Valencia's website to have a more simplified and modular layout
  2. Improve the mobile experience and implement responsive design
  3. Restructure content to reinforce a clearer messaging hierarchy

Discover

During the research phase, I conducted a competitive analysis and distributed online surveys in order to help define 826 Valencia's target audience, understand the organization's priorities, and evaluate similar organizations in the existing space. Since the organization's reach was broad, I created and distributed 3 different online surveys to the following user groups: teachers, potential volunteers/donators, and general users.

Key Takeaways

  • The organization's strategy deck and input from an 826 staff member helped to hone in on focus areas — growing the volunteer/donor base and outreach to teachers.
  • Many participants of the general user survey described the site as busy and confusing. There was too much information and content.
  • Some of the top content both teachers and potential volunteers/donors wanted to see on a non-profit site included:
    1. Details on programs & services
    2. Detailed information about what the org does
    3. Statistics on the impact of the organization
    4. Photos of workshops/tutoring sessions in action

Define

I compiled my research and created a UX strategy blueprint to define the challenges of the project and devise ways to overcome them. Based on this strategy, I created a sitemap to restructure the site's content in a way that made most sense, paying careful attention to the Volunteer and Donation pages.

User Flows

Based on the focus areas from my research and UX strategy blueprint, I mapped out user flows for the following scenarios: 1) a person who is interested in volunteering (pictured below); 2) a person who is interested in donating; 3) a teacher who is interested in programs and parterning with 826 Valencia.

Design

Wireframes

I created low-fidelity wireframes in Sketch, focusing in on the pages relevant to my user flows — mainly the homepage and any pages involved within the volunteer, donation, and teacher flows.


Test

In order to validate my initial design asssumptions, I created a prototype through InVision and a test with 3 main tasks centering around my main user flows. The prototype was tested on 3 participants in person, and a navigation test was conducted on UsabilityHub.

Results

Results for the navigation test and in-person testing indicated flows were headed in the right direction. Overall, users were able to complete tasks quite easily with a 100% completion rate for in-person testing. Also, participants were actually scanning the page, looking through the content, which indicated they were intrigued by the content and that the information included was useful for them.

Deliver

Brand Style Tile

Building off of 826 Valencia's core brand styles, I wanted to explore different design ideas. Ultimately, I decided to stick with the organization's original logo (which represents the top of its HQ/Pirate Storefront). I made some adjustments to the color palette, typography, and icon styles in order to play up the pirate theme, and, at the same time, add some vibrancy and modernity to the site's overall look.

Conclusion

Lessons Learned

Overall, this was an interesting and fun project to work on, given 826 Valencia's mission and creative pirate theme. Although, I did get to work on some of the brand's visual elements, the bulk of this project was spent on thinking through the information architecture. I learned that, even if a company has strong graphical elements, it still needs a solid site structure, navigation, and content strategy to create an overall good user experience.

This was also one of my first projects, where I had to compile feedback and data from multiple target audiences. Since the nature of the non-profits' reach is so broad, this project forced me to really think about how to integrate all these different points of views and desires into a cohesive site experience.

Next Steps

If given more time on this project, I would take the following next steps:
  • A/B test the screens involved in the teacher flow for futher optimization
  • Work on optimizing the individual flows for students and volunteers who register on the website — including the onboarding process and steps taken to sign up for a workshop/tutoring session
