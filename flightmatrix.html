American Airlines Vacations

Switchfly Project:
A New Enhanced Air Shopping Experience

Overview

Switchfly is a white label platform that specializes in building loyalty and travel package solutions for airlines and financial companies. Longtime client American Airlines came with a request to create a flight shopping experience for their vacation website that would provide full visibility to their wide range of cabin and fare offerings.

The Challenge

At the time, the product cards on our flight results page only displayed 1 type of cabin class. The experience for AA would require a completely new interface to be built into the platform that could support multiple cabin classes and fare products.



(Standard Switchfly flight product card)

My Role

I was the sole designer for this project and oversaw the end-to-end design process, where I was in charge of research, user testing, user experience and interface design, prototyping, and outlining specifications. My team consisted of a product manager and several engineers.

Project Goals

Taking into consideration the clientâ€™s needs and our own business goals, the main objectives of this project were to:

  1. Create a new interface that could support multiple layers of cabin class & fare options to drive upsell opportunities.
  2. Create a scalable and web accessible product that would fit within our platform and increase likelhood for client adoption.
  3. Create a differentiated product within the travel industry marketplace that would get clients excited and act as a selling point to attract new customers.

Discover

During the research phase of this project, I conducted an extensive competitor analysis, reviewing 10 other airlines websites to collect and compare data of those with similar multiple travel class and fare display structures.

Key Takeaways

From my competitor analysis, I found that:

  • Most competitors usually laid out travel class and fare options in a horizontal, grid-like format.
  • Many had strong messaging hierarchies, where price, time, and airport code tended to stand out.
  • I observed in what ways we could incorporate a more modern look and feel to our current app. I noticed that many did this through the thoughtful use of standardized spacing and color, as well as careful detail to font styles.

Define

Project Scope

For this project, I worked very closely with the product manager to understand all the business requirements, as well as our engineers to understand the technical constraints and accessibility considerations. Together, we worked on defining the project's scope. As the designer, I provided input on UI-related items, such as responsivenesss and interactivity, as well as helped to identify what new components we would need to have built.

Test

Designing this feature for desktop was one thing, but handheld proved to be even more of a challenge. How would we be able to condense things even more, but still maintain a logical flow? I decided on a direction for handheld, but I felt it was necessary to conduct a usability test to validate if the design worked. I then followed that with a preference test to pin down the final details of the handheld design.


(Prototype created in Proto.io

Testing Results

I tested my prototype on a total of 20 participants. Some of the key takeaways from testing included:
  • The majority of users were able to complete tasks and get through the prototype easily. The nature of the multiple fare levels was complicated, but most users were able to understand the flow.
  • In general, when users are shopping for flights, the following are the most important factors: price, times, and number of stops.
  • We learned there could be more clarity when the user opened the cabin drawer. Also, we learned that users liked seeing layover information outright.

Conclusion

Results

The feature went live on June 13, 2019 (see it in action on the AAVacations website). It has resulted in the following:
  • Another major client Copa Airlines will be going live with the feature next year in 2020.
  • Our sales team has started incorporating the feature into demos and it has become a huge selling point for recently-acquired clients (Japan Airlines and JetBlue).
  • The feature has generated ~8,300+ bookings so far.
