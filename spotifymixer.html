Spotify Mixer

Designlab UX Academy Project:
New Feature to Share Music with Friends

Overview

Background

Spotify is a digital music service that gives users access to millions of songs and lets them listen to music wherever they are in the world. Though ubiquitous and a monument to "good enough," Spotify's experience isn't what it could be. The service offers basic social features, such as sharing playlists and the ability to follow friends & artists, but there's definitely room for improvement.

My main goals of this capstone project were to:

  1. Review competitors and investigate the ways people share and listen to music together
  2. Design a new social feature within an existing system
  3. Learn how to use Framer to build a working prototype

Discover

During the research phase, I wanted to focus on identifying the different ways in which people usually shared music and what pain points came along with it. I chose to conduct a competitve analysis, as well as in-person interviews and an online survey to help me better understand the social experience of digital music services.

Key Takeaways

When sharing music with friends, most participants mentioned that they would copy a link and paste it to a text or different social app. Frustrations were mostly related to sharing through different platforms/competing services and not being a seamless, integrated experience. All music services reviewed in the competitive analysis had similar sharing functionality, but one thing seemed to be lacking across the board — a way to share music with others within the app.

Define

During my user interviews, a popular feature suggestion brought up was the ability to group chat with friends. Based on what I saw in the competitive analysis and the frustrations brought up during my primary research, this direction seemed like a good way to go. Thus, Spotify Mixer was born — playing on the idea of a party mixer and a music mix, this feature would be a combination of a group chatroom and collaborative playlist.

I built personas and a sitemap to help me refine the strategy and focus of my new social feature.

Personas

Two types of personas emerged from my primary research — "Madison the Music Aficiando" and "Carl the Casual Listener". These personas helped me to empathize with key users and guide my decision-making on ensuing design.

Test


Prototype created in Framer

In order to arrive on the best solution for my high-fidelity wireframes and prototype, I planned out a couple of rounds of testing.

Iteration & Prototype

I distributed my first round of tests on UsabilityHub to get a gauge on my initial wireframes. I took the results from my first round of testing and began building the high-fidelity wireframes and prototype. I saw this project as a good opportunity to learn how to use the Framer tool and used it to create my prototype.

Usability Testing Results

I tested my prototype on 3 participants. Overall, there was a high completion rate of 95%, which indicated that the design and flow were headed in the right direction. Most confusion seemed to lie in the limitations of the prototype and initial understanding of the new feature itself.

Conclusion

Lessons Learned

This was one of the first projects during my time in Designlab, where I had to design something within an existing system. When I was initially thinking through the UI, I tried to pull in different ideas from various social apps. Ultimately, what worked best was pulling from Spotify's own existing design patterns.

In the end, I learned that when it comes to design, one doesn't need to completely reinvent the wheel with every project. Sometimes, the best solution can stem from embracing patterns that have been proven to work and creating something new from that process. Also, it was helpful to have these brand, UI, and interactive elements already in place to allow me to focus on other things like learning a new tool — Framer.

Next Steps

If given more time on this project, I would take the following next steps:
  • Build addtional custom interactions within the high-fidelity Framer prototype
  • Further optimize the flow to create a new Mixer group through A/B testing different versions of the screens involved
  • Work on an onboarding flow to give users a better idea of how the feature works
